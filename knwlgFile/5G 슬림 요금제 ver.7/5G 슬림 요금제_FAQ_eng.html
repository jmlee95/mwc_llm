 FAQ
 

Q1. How can I check my data usage for Data Together/Tethering/Data Sharing?
A1. You can check it on the KOS/My usage inquiry page, and you will receive a real-time usage text message when your usage is exhausted.

Q2. Does the 5G Slim QoS plan provide data push-up service?
A2. No. The 5G Slim QoS plan does not provide carryover and pull-up services as it is an unlimited QoS plan.

 

[24.01.18 Version Upgrade Content 4 ] Added Q3~Q8

Q3. Will I be charged excess fees when using shared data after using up the basic data for the rollover/volume rate plan?

A3. No, the speed is controlled at 200Kbps and no overage charges are incurred. (Same as QoS type rate plan)

 

Q4. When changing between a rollover/volume rate plan and an LTE per-month rate plan (such as LTE Basic), will the rollover data be carried over?

A4. No, it will not be carried over except for the 5G rollover/change between 5 types of pay-as-you-go rate plans. 

 

Q5. Is the discount for the optional contract applied to excess charges incurred while using the rollover/volume rate plan?

A5. No, it does not apply. 

 

Q6. How can customers find out if there are any excess charges and the amount of excess charges while using the rollover/volume rate plan?

A6. A notification text message will be sent when 50%/80%/100% of the basic data provided has been used up and when the excess amount exceeds 10,000/20,000/30,000/40,000/60,000/80,000 won. 

     Customers can also check directly through the My or My KT app on kt.com. 

 

Q7. Is it only possible to lift speed control (cancel '5G data excess charge cap' additional service) in the 3rd section of the pay-as-you-go billing plan for rollover/pay-as-you-go plans on KOS?

A7. No, customers can sign up/cancel their subscription directly through the My or My KT app on kt.com. 

 

Q8. When changing between a QoS-type rate plan with the same monthly fee and a rollover/volume rate plan, is it included in the number of rate plan changes during the month? 

A8. Yes, QoS rate plans and rollover/volume rate plans are included in the number of changes to separate rate plans.
